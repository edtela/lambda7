The Proton Mass

\[ m_p = 6\pi^5 + \frac{4}{5}e^{-\pi} \]

Full Calculation

\( 6\pi^5 \) = \( 6 \times 306.0196848 \) = 1836.1181 \( m_e \)
\( \frac{4}{5}e^{-\pi} \) = \( 0.8 \times 0.04321392 \) = 0.0346 \( m_e \)
Sum = 1836.1527 \( m_e \)
Mass = \( 1836.1527 \times 0.51099895 \text{ MeV} \) = 938.2721 MeV
Experimental = 938.2720888 MeV

Error: 3 eV  |  0.003 ppm

The proton mass, expressed in electron mass units, equals six times pi to the fifth power, plus a small Lorentz correction. This formula matches experiment to within 3 electronvolts.

The Derivative Framework

Why can we differentiate π? In the radial singularity picture, π is not just a constant — it represents the radial coordinate \(r\) evaluated at \(r = \pi\):

\[ f(r) = r^n \quad \Rightarrow \quad f(\pi) = \pi^n, \quad f'(\pi) = n\pi^{n-1} \]

Baryon masses emerge as derivatives of source functions:

Proton (Q = +1)

\[ \text{Source: } \pi^6 \] \[ \frac{d}{d\pi}(\pi^6) = 6\pi^5 \]

Correction: \(+\frac{4}{5}e^{-\pi}\)

Neutron (Q = 0)

\[ \text{Source: } \pi^6 + 8\ln\pi \] \[ \frac{d}{d\pi} = 6\pi^5 + \frac{8}{\pi} \]

Correction: built into source

Omega (Q = -1)

\[ \text{Source: } \frac{3}{2}\pi^6 \] \[ \frac{d}{d\pi}\left(\frac{3}{2}\pi^6\right) = 9\pi^5 \]

Correction: \(+\frac{6}{5}e^{-\pi}\)

The proton and omega form a duality across the charge spectrum:

  • Proton (Q = +1): \(6\pi^5 + \frac{4}{5}e^{-\pi}\) — uses \(\frac{4}{5} = \frac{\lfloor[2]_\pi\rfloor}{5}\)
  • Omega (Q = -1): \(9\pi^5 + \frac{6}{5}e^{-\pi}\) — uses \(\frac{6}{5} = \frac{\lfloor 2\pi \rfloor}{5}\)
  • Neutron (Q = 0): \(6\pi^5 + \frac{8}{\pi}\) — hides \(\frac{4}{5}\) in 8 = \(\frac{4}{5} \times \lceil\pi^2\rceil\)

The correction factors \(\frac{4}{5}\) and \(\frac{6}{5}\) sum to 2. Both use denominator 5 = \(\lceil[2]_\pi\rceil\), with numerators from the floor operations on \([2]_\pi\) and \(2\pi\). The charged particles (proton, omega) need external Lorentz corrections; the neutral neutron absorbs its correction into the logarithmic source.
