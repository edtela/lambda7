The Charm Cycle

Charm baryons at \( [3]_\pi = 14 \)

Overview

The charm baryon cycle begins at a different base than the strange cycle. Where strange baryons use \( c_5 = 6 = \lfloor 2\pi \rfloor \), charm baryons use the third q-integer:

\[ c_5 = 14 = [3]_\pi = \pi^2 + \pi + 1 \approx 14.01 \]

The q-calculus connection suggests charm baryons live in a discrete mathematical structure at base π, where q-integers replace ordinary integers.

Double-Charm (π⁶ level)

Double-charm baryons live at the π⁶ level. The base coefficient 7 from strange S=-1 gets multiplied by π when projected to the π⁵ world:

\[ 7\pi^6 = 7\pi \times \pi^5 \approx 22\pi^5 \]

The q-Integer Connection

The q-integers at base \(\pi\) form a sequence that appears to organize baryon masses:

\[ [n]_\pi = \frac{\pi^n - 1}{\pi - 1} = \pi^{n-1} + \pi^{n-2} + \cdots + \pi + 1 \]
\([1]_\pi\) = 1 (trivial)
\([2]_\pi\) = \(\pi + 1 \approx 4.14\) (appears in \(\Xi^-\) correction)
\([3]_\pi\) = \(\pi^2 + \pi + 1 \approx 14.01\) Charm base coefficient
\([4]_\pi\) = \(\pi^3 + \pi^2 + \pi + 1 \approx 45.02\)

The near-integer values of \([n]_\pi\) may explain why particle masses can be expressed as polynomials in \(\pi\) with small integer coefficients.
