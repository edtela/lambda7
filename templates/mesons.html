Mesons & Leptons

Light particle masses from π-polynomials with Lorentz corrections

The Pion

The charged pion — the lightest meson — follows the same π-polynomial structure as baryons, with a Lorentz correction using \(e^{-\pi}\):

\[ m_{\pi^\pm} = \pi^5 - \pi^3 - \pi + 1 + 6e^{-\pi} \]

Full Calculation

\( \pi^5 \) = 306.0197 \( m_e \)
\( -\pi^3 \) = -31.0063 \( m_e \)
\( -\pi \) = -3.1416 \( m_e \)
\( +1 \) = 1.0000 \( m_e \)
\( 6e^{-\pi} \) = 0.2593 \( m_e \)
Sum = 273.1311 \( m_e \)
Mass = 139.570 MeV
Experimental = 139.570 MeV

Error: 0.3 keV  |  0.002 ppm

The Coefficient 6

The Lorentz correction coefficient \(6 = \lfloor 2\pi \rfloor\) is the same as the proton's leading coefficient. This connects the pion to the nucleon through the floor function:

Proton

\[ 6\pi^5 + \frac{4}{5}e^{-\pi} \]

Leading coefficient = 6

Pion±

\[ \pi^5 - \pi^3 - \pi + 1 + 6e^{-\pi} \]

Lorentz coefficient = 6

The Neutral Pion

The neutral pion has a slightly different structure, with all dimensions represented:

\[ m_{\pi^0} = \pi^5 - \pi^3 - \pi^2 - 1 \]

Calculation

\( \pi^5 - \pi^3 - \pi^2 - 1 \) = 264.1518 \( m_e \)
Mass = 134.98 MeV
Experimental = 134.977 MeV

Error: 3 keV

The Unit Vector of π-Space

The neutral pion can be viewed as a "characteristic polynomial" or unit vector of \(\pi^6\) space. It contains all the independent lower dimensions with unit coefficients:

\[ \pi^0 \sim \pi^5 - \pi^3 - \pi^2 - 1 \]

The "defects" at \(\pi^3\), \(\pi^2\), and \(\pi^0\) represent the structure that gets filled in when strangeness is added (see the Seven Connection).

The Muon

The muon — the "heavy electron" — follows the same Lorentz correction pattern:

\[ m_\mu = \pi^5 - \pi^4 - 1 - 20e^{-\pi} \]

Full Calculation

\( \pi^5 \) = 306.0197 \( m_e \)
\( -\pi^4 \) = -97.4091 \( m_e \)
\( -1 \) = -1.0000 \( m_e \)
\( -20e^{-\pi} \) = -0.8643 \( m_e \)
Sum = 206.746 \( m_e \)
Mass = 105.647 MeV
Experimental = 105.658 MeV

Error: 11 keV  |  0.01%

The Coefficient -20

The Lorentz correction coefficient \(-20 = -4 \times 5\) comes from the magnetic moment family. This is the same "4×5 = 20" that appears in the Omega baryon's correction:

Particle Lorentz Coefficient Family
Proton \( +\frac{4}{5} \) 4×5 (uses fraction)
Omega \( +\frac{6}{5} \) 2×3 (cross-reference)
Pion± \( +6 \) 2×3 (proton family)
Muon \( -20 \) 4×5 (full integer)

Pion-Muon Relationship

The pion and muon formulas differ only in the \(\pi^4\) vs \(\pi^3\) term and the sign/magnitude of the Lorentz correction:

Pion±

\[ \pi^5 - \pi^3 - \pi + 1 + 6e^{-\pi} \]

139.57 MeV

Muon

\[ \pi^5 - \pi^4 - 1 - 20e^{-\pi} \]

105.65 MeV

This reflects the physical relationship: the charged pion decays almost exclusively to a muon (\(\pi^+ \to \mu^+ + \nu_\mu\)). The formulas share the same \(\pi^5\) base but use different dimensional corrections.

The \(c_5\) Pattern

The leading coefficient reveals the quark content:

Mesons \(c_5\) Pattern
\( \pi \) (pion) 1 Lightest meson
\( K, \eta \) 3 One strange quark
\( \rho, \omega \) 5 Vector mesons (J=1)
\( \eta', \phi \) 6 Heavier strange
\( D \) 12 = 2×6 Charm + light
\( D_s \) 13 = 12+1 Charm + strange
\( J/\psi \) 20 = 4×5 Charm-anticharm
\( B \) 34 = 6²-2 Bottom + light
\( \Upsilon \) 61 = 64-3 Bottom-antibottom
