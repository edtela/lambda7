Three Interlocking Frameworks

The π-algebra emerges from three independent mathematical structures that converge on the same particle coefficients:

The Lorentz Correction

\[ e^{-\pi} = 0.0432... \]

The discrete-continuous duality creates corrections proportional to \(e^{-\pi}\). This "antimatter shadow" appears in the proton formula with coefficient 4/5.

  • Arises from summing vs integrating dimensional series
  • Connected to Lorentz boosts at rapidity \(\eta = \pi\)
  • The 4/5 comes from floor/ceiling of \([2]_\pi\)

q-Calculus with q = π

\[ [n]_\pi = \frac{\pi^n - 1}{\pi - 1} = 1 + \pi + \pi^2 + \cdots + \pi^{n-1} \]

The π-integers give the dimension-spanning coefficients for heavy quarks. The charm coefficient 14 equals \([3]_\pi\) to 0.08% accuracy.

  • \([1]_\pi = 1\) (u,d quarks)
  • \([2]_\pi = \pi + 1 \approx 4.14\) (strange)
  • \([3]_\pi = \pi^2 + \pi + 1 \approx 14.01\) (charm)
  • \([4]_\pi \approx 45.02\) (bottom)

The Seven Connection

\[ 7 = \lceil 2\pi \rceil = \lfloor \pi! \rfloor = \lfloor \pi^2 + \pi - 6 \rfloor \]

The strange baryon coefficient 7 emerges from three independent calculations: the ceiling of 2π, the π-factorial, and a quadratic in π.

  • Proton: \(\lfloor 2\pi \rfloor = 6\)
  • Strange: \(\lceil 2\pi \rceil = 7\)
  • Omega: \(\lceil \pi^2 \rceil = 10\)

The Unified Picture

These frameworks connect through key identities:

The Fundamental Identity

\[ [2]_\pi + (\pi - 1) = 2\pi \]

This explains why both proton (6) and strange baryons (7) derive from the same expression with different quantizations.

The Charm Identity

\[ [3]_\pi = \pi^2 + \pi + 1 = 14.01 \approx 2 \times 7 \]

The charm coefficient equals twice the strange coefficient — charm is "two units of strangeness."

The Double Charm Identity

\[ e^\pi = 23.14 \approx 23 = 2 \times [3]_\pi - \lceil [2]_\pi \rceil \]

The double-charm coefficient comes from \(e^\pi\), connecting to the Lorentz framework.
