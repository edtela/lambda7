The Bottom Cycle

Bottom baryons at \( c_5 = 36 = 6^2 \)

Overview

The bottom baryon cycle begins at \( c_5 = 36 \), which has a special interpretation:

\[ c_5 = 36 = 6^2 = \text{(proton coefficient)}^2 \]

Like the strange cycle, bottom baryons follow the strangeness rule \( c_5 = 36 + |S| \), and the charge window mirrors the strange baryons exactly (no shift, unlike charm).

The 6² Connection

The base coefficient 36 = 6² suggests a "squaring" relationship between the proton (6π&sup5;) and bottom baryons (36π&sup5;):

\[ \text{Bottom base} = (\text{Proton coefficient})^2 = 6^2 = 36 \]

Alternatively, we can view 36 ≈ 4π² ≈ 39.5, which would give:

\[ 36\pi^5 \approx 4\pi^7 \]

This suggests bottom baryons might also be understood as living at the π&sup7; level, similar to how double-charm baryons (22π&sup5; ≈ 7π&sup6;) bridge power levels.

The Universal k/5 Pattern

All corrections across strange, charm, and bottom cycles use the k/5 denominator:

  • Strange: k/5 with e-π terms
  • Charm: k/5 with π terms
  • Bottom: k/5 with π terms (simpler, no e-π)
